peanut butter cookies

these cookies really shine with virgin coconut oil! refined won't give the same flavor; in that case you might as well use butter. the pb:oil ratio is based on Earth Balance coconut & peanut spread, which you can use to replace them both if you want, if you can find it; in that case you will probably want to add less (or no) salt, and less sugar since it's already sweetened.

these don't need gluten to hold the batter together, so i usually make them using non-wheat flour of some kind. teff and buckwheat both give a very nice color contrast to the peanut chunks. but you can use regular flour too, of course.

if you don't have turbinado sugar for the topping, you can use brown sugar, or just skip it.

ingredients

wet

  • 240g crunchy unsalted peanut butter
  • 57g virgin coconut oil, melted
  • 267g cane sugar
  • 120g milklike

dry

  • 200g flour (GF preferred)
  • 8g cinnamon
  • 6g baking powder
  • 6g salt

topping

  • turbinado sugar

directions

mix wet and dry ingredients in separate bowls. add wet to dry and mix until incorporated. form into balls and flatten on a parchment paper-lined cookie sheet. use a fork or whisk or pastry blender to impress cross hatches on top, and sprinkle with turbinado sugar. bake 13m at 350F.
